Exploring Road Crashes In Australia and How Alcohol Contributes to Such Issues

Map: Alcohol Consumption Related Death Rates (2023)

This choropleth map displays fatal road crash rates across Australian states and territories. Darker shades indicate higher death rates per 100,000 people. The Northern Territory and Queensland record the highest rates, reflecting extensive rural networks and higher speed zones.

Crash Type and Speed Limit Analysis (click legend to filter)

Boxplot: Distribution of Speed Limits

The boxplot compares posted speed limits at fatal crash sites across states. Northern Territory and South Australia show wider spreads, while Victoria and Tasmania cluster around moderate speeds, suggesting controlled environments.

Area Chart: Crash Type Comparison (1989–2022)

The area chart shows a major long-term decline in both single- and multi-vehicle fatal crashes since the early 1990s. However, single-vehicle crashes remain proportionally consistent, reflecting ongoing behavioral risks.

Line Chart: Fatal Crashes and Deaths (2019–2023)

The line chart illustrates how both fatal crashes and total deaths rose steadily until 2022 before slightly declining in 2023. The consistent gap between the lines suggests multi-fatality incidents still contribute significantly to total deaths.

Heatmap: Fatal Crashes by Day of the Week

This heatmap reveals distinct temporal crash patterns. Friday and Saturday show darker shades, highlighting peak risk from social activities and fatigue. Early weekdays remain lower risk.

Crashes by Hour and Day

The faceted area plot visualizes hourly crash frequencies. Late evenings, especially weekends, show peaks linked to fatigue and low visibility — crucial for timing road-safety patrols.
