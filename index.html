Exploring Road Crashes In Australia and How Alcohol contribute to such issue

Map: Fatal Road Crash Rates in Australia (2023)

This choropleth map displays the fatal road crash rates across Australian states and territories in 2023. Darker shades indicate higher death rates per 100,000 people. The Northern Territory and Queensland record the highest rates, reflecting extensive rural networks and higher speed zones.

Crash Type and Speed Limit Analysis

Boxplot: Distribution of Speed Limits

The boxplot compares posted speed limits at fatal crash sites across states. The Northern Territory and South Australia show wider spreads, reflecting mixed urban–rural road conditions, while Victoria and Tasmania concentrate around mid-range speeds, suggesting crashes occur primarily in controlled speed environments.

Area Chart: Crash Type Comparison (1989–2022)

The area chart reveals a major long-term decline in both single- and multi-vehicle fatal crashes since the early 1990s. However, single-vehicle crashes remain proportionally persistent, showing that driver behaviour factors—fatigue, distraction, or impairment—continue to dominate fatality risks despite overall safety gains.

Line Chart: Fatal Crashes and Deaths (2019–2023)

The line chart illustrates how both fatal crashes and total deaths rose steadily until 2022 before showing a slight decline in 2023. The consistent gap between the two lines indicates that multi-fatality incidents contribute heavily to total deaths, emphasizing the importance of reducing not just crash frequency, but also crash severity.

Heatmap: Fatal Crashes by Day of the Week

This heatmap reveals distinct temporal patterns in crash fatalities. Friday and Saturday show the darkest cells, marking peak risk periods often linked to late-night travel, social activities, and fatigue. In contrast, Monday–Wednesday remain relatively low, indicating predictable weekly rhythms useful for targeted enforcement strategies.
